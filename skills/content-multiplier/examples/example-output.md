# Example: Content Multiplier Output

## Source Content

> "We spent 3 years building a SaaS product before realizing our best feature was the one we almost cut. Our analytics dashboard was an afterthought — built in 2 days during a hackathon. But it became the #1 reason customers stayed. Lesson: listen to usage data, not your roadmap."

## X/Twitter Thread

**1/6**
We almost killed the feature that saved our company.

3 years building a SaaS product. Dozens of features planned. But the one that mattered most? Built in 2 days during a hackathon.

Here's the story:

**2/6**
Our analytics dashboard was an afterthought.

It wasn't on the roadmap. No one championed it. An engineer built it over a weekend because they were curious.

We almost didn't ship it.

**3/6**
6 months later, we looked at the data.

The dashboard was the #1 reason customers renewed. Not the features we spent years perfecting.

The "throwaway" feature was carrying the business.

**4/6**
The mistake we made:

We listened to our roadmap instead of our users. We built what we thought they needed, not what they actually used.

**5/6**
The lesson:

Your best feature might already exist — buried in your product, ignored by your team.

Look at usage data. What are people actually doing?

**6/6**
Stop building what you planned.

Start building what people use.

Your roadmap is a guess. Your data is the truth.
